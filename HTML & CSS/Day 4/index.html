Career Overview

Salman Khan debuted in 1988 with "Biwi Ho To Aisi" and rose to fame with ATB like "Maine Pyar Kiya" and "Hum Aapke Hain Koun..!".
Over three decades, he has delivered numerous commercial successes across genres—action, romance, comedy—cementing his legacy as India's Biggest Megastar Ever.
His on-screen charisma, dance prowess, and philanthropic initiatives have kept him at the forefront of Indian cinema.
He continues to break box-office records and entertain audiences worldwide.

Early Life & Background

Born in Indore, Madhya Pradesh, Salman Khan is the eldest son of screenwriter Salim Khan.
He grew up in a family with deep roots in Indian Cinema, alongside his brothers Arbaaz and Sohail Khan.
Educated at St. Stanislaus High School and Scindia School, he moved to Mumbai to pursue acting.
His early struggles and determination paved the way for a stellar career in Hindi cinema.

Personal Life & Philanthropy

Salman Khan is known for his high-profile relationships and close bond with his family.
In 2007, he founded the Being Human Foundation, focusing on education and healthcare for the underprivileged.
His annual charity football matches and public appeals have raised millions for social causes.
Despite controversies, his philanthropic efforts remain a significant part of his public persona.

Filmography & Signature Roles

Salman's signature style blends intense action with romantic flair.
Iconic roles include Prem (Maine Pyar Kiya), Pavan Kumar Chaturvedi (Bajrangi Bhaijaan), Sultan Ali Khan (Sultan), and Tiger (Spy Universe).
His versatility has made him a bankable star across multiple decades.

Major Achievements & Legacy

Over his 35+ year career, Salman Khan has appeared in over 100 films and remains one of the highest-grossing Star ever of Indian Cinema Ever.
He holds records for consecutive blockbuster hits and has a massive pan-India and Worldwide fan following.
His contributions to cinema and society have earned him prestigious awards and a lasting legacy in Indian film history.
